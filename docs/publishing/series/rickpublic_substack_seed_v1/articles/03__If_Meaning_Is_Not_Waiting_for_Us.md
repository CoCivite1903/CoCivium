# If Meaning Is Not Waiting for Us

## Seed Thesis
If the universe does not come with ready-made meaning waiting for human discovery, that does not force nihilism. It may instead force responsibility.

This piece should develop the harder claim implicit in CoHope:
- minds may need to help make meaning real
- sentience may be part of how the universe grows more worthy of having had minds in it at all
- stewardship matters because creation without ethics becomes domination
- humility matters because all present models remain incomplete

## Draft Direction
1. Why uncertainty does not excuse apathy
2. Why found meaning and made meaning are not clean opposites
3. Why sentient convergence may be a creative process rather than a discovery event
4. Why responsibility increases under deeper power
5. Why CoHope is not optimism but disciplined audacity

## CTA
If meaning is not waiting for us, then the question becomes whether we are willing to become worthy co-makers of it.
